Question: In a recent case in New South Wales, SafeWork NSW prosecuted XYZ Constructions Pty Ltd and its manager, Jane Roe, after an incident where a worker was injured due to lack of fall protection measures during a construction project. The company was found guilty of failing to adhere to the guidelines set by the SafeWork NSW Code of Practice for Construction Work. Jane Roe was also found responsible for not providing adequate supervision and training to the workers. As a result, the court imposed a fine of $80,000 on XYZ Constructions Pty Ltd and a $25,000 fine on Jane Roe. The case served as a reminder of the obligations under the Work Health and Safety Act 2011 to ensure a safe working environment and the importance of managerial oversight. How did XYZ Constructions Pty Ltd fail in terms of regulatory compliance?

cot:
To determine how XYZ Constructions Pty Ltd failed in terms of regulatory compliance, we need to analyze the components of the case presented.

1. **Understanding the Incident**: The report indicates that a worker was injured due to a lack of fall protection measures during a construction project. This directly points to a safety issue on site, specifically related to fall protection, which is a critical aspect of workplace safety in construction.

2. **Legal Framework**: The prosecution occurred under the Work Health and Safety Act 2011, which emphasizes the employer's obligation to ensure a safe work environment. The court also referenced the SafeWork NSW Code of Practice for Construction Work, which outlines specific measures and practices that must be followed, including adequate safety measures for falls.

3. **Conclusion**: Given the analysis, the clear failure of XYZ Constructions Pty Ltd regarding regulatory compliance pertains to the safety incident related to fall protection measures. Therefore, the correct answer is:

** By failing to provide appropriate fall protection measures.**
