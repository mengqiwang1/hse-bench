According to OSHA standards, what is the primary reason for mandating the use of safety helmets on construction sites?
Option:
A. To improve worker morale.
B. To provide protection against electrical shock.
C. To protect against impact and falling debris.
D. To satisfy insurance requirements.
answer: C
cot:
To determine the correct answer to why OSHA mandates the use of safety helmets on construction sites, let's analyze each option in context:

A. **To improve worker morale.** While morale can be an important aspect of workplace safety and culture, safety helmets are not primarily intended to boost morale. Their usage is based on the protection they provide.

B. **To provide protection against electrical shock.** Safety helmets offer some general electrical safety features, but their primary function is not specifically to protect against electrical shock. Other personal protective equipment (PPE) is usually employed for that specific risk.

C. **To protect against impact and falling debris.** This option aligns with the primary function of safety helmets. Construction sites often involve overhead work where tools, materials, or debris can fall, posing a significant risk of head injury. Helmets are specifically designed to absorb impact and protect workers from such hazards.

D. **To satisfy insurance requirements.** While compliance with safety regulations can help in satisfying insurance requirements, the underlying reason for requiring safety helmets relates to actual worker safety rather than simply adhering to policy or insurance guidelines.

Considering these points, the most accurate reason for enforcing the use of safety helmets in construction settings, supported by OSHA standards, is that they are essential for **protecting against impact and falling debris (option C).** This clearly addresses the dangers present on a construction site and substantiates the requirement for this type of personal protective equipment.
